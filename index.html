Optimization Timeline & Progression

The policy optimization shows a classic Pareto trade-off between **aggressive early gate clears** and **back-half safety/precision**. Early iterations cleared up to 3.25 raw gates by using a high-energy launch, but over-energized the ball, preventing gate 3-4 clearances and leading to catastrophic crashes. Later iterations traded early raw gate speed for controlled window threading and target landings.

Safety vs. Window Trade-offs
Progression Analysis: - Epoch 0-15 (Exploration): Initiated with extremely high launch velocity, securing 2.75 raw gates and 2.00 bounced gates on average, but safety remained at 0.0 due to extreme recovery attitude tilt spikes (up to 180 degrees). - Epoch 16-30 (Safety-first Search): Tuned the attitude tilt limiter and restricted collective thrust. Safety peaked at 0.451 at Epoch 26. This limited the drone to 2.25 raw gates but completely prevented drone/gate collisions. - Epoch 31-49 (Precision & Dwell): Prioritized clean window passes. Bounced gate count settled at 1.00, but window passes climbed to a perfect 1.00 average, proving the viability of a smoother, less aggressive trajectory profile.
